what it is

a notes app i actually use

it's a notes list, an editor, an artifact panel and a chat, all running over a real folder of markdown that Claude can actually read.

01 / notes

notes

it's a Monaco editor (the same one inside VS Code) hooked up to a real folder of .md files. it auto-saves on every keystroke and there's no weird proprietary format, just your files.

02 / claude

claude, built in

the chat lives in the right panel and streams as it goes, with a model picker, a thinking toggle and a stop button. you can keep multiple chats per folder and they all save locally.

03 / artifacts

artifacts

Claude can make and edit artifacts like code, mermaid diagrams and structured output. they get rendered, you can zoom in on them, and they save per folder.

what claude can see

it works your actual folder, not a sandbox

every tool is scoped to the folder you opened, and nothing leaves it.

your data

it's local-first and it never phones home

CATS has no server, no account and no cloud sync. i want to be straight about what that does and doesn't mean.

stays on your pc

your notes, chats and artifacts are just files in the folder you picked. CATS never uploads them or syncs them anywhere, because there's no CATS server to sync to.

the one thing that leaves

when you actually ask Claude something, that message goes straight from your computer to Anthropic's API using your own key. CATS is not a middleman, and i never see it. that part is just how any cloud AI works.

what anthropic does with it

Anthropic doesn't train their models on what you send through the API, and they only hold onto it briefly to watch for abuse. nothing you write gets baked into a model.
